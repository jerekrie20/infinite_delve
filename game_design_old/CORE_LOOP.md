# Core loop (DELVE)

**Related:** [[mechanics-index]] · [[classes]] · [[frontier]] · [[Home]]

*Drafted 2026-07-08. The skeleton the mechanics hang on. Numbers are
placeholders to make it concrete — all tunable later.*

## The shape in one breath

**Solo in the small, together in the large.** Your minute-to-minute is a solo
run; your day-to-day and season are shared with the whole sub. The two never
fight because they live at different scales.

---

## 1. Session loop — one delve (~5–15 min)

The thing you actually do when you open the post.

1. **Open** → see your hero, your gear, the sub's frontier progress, and
   today's delve waiting.
2. **Enter today's delve** — a procedurally-generated island/dungeon under fog
   (server-generated from the daily seed; everyone in the sub faces the same
   day's challenge, but plays it in their own instance).
3. **Explore & fight** — turn-based / light-action: move the hero, peel back
   fog, fight Tiny Swords monsters, open chests, pick up loot (weapons, armor,
   abilities — free sprites).
4. **Push or bank** — the roguelike heartbeat: go deeper for better loot, or
   **extract** to lock in what you've got. Deeper = richer = riskier.
5. **Resolve** — extract safely → loot banked, hero gains XP; **die** → lose
   the *unbanked* loot this run (stakes), but the **hero persists** (RPG).
6. **Contribute & hook** — your run's results roll into the sub's frontier
   (tiles explored, boss damage, resources). Close on a hook: *"the sub is 60%
   to the Sunken City"* / *"you're 1 level from the next ability."*

**This delivers:** the blueprint's *Agency* ("my run mattered") and *Drama*
("did I bank it or bust?") at the individual level — the exact thing Faction
War was missing.

---

## 2. Daily loop

- **New delve seed each day** = fresh content = the reason to return = the
  Reddit DQE (daily-qualified-engager) money mechanic.
- **Run budget** (e.g. 1–3 delves/day, refreshes daily) bounds engagement,
  creates scarcity, and quietly caps whales so time-rich ≠ pay-to-win.
- **Daily community tick** (server scheduler): aggregates yesterday's runs,
  advances the shared frontier, may spawn a world event / boss, and auto-posts
  a **"frontier report"** (reused from Faction War's war-report concept) — free
  content + comment fuel.
- **Comments** = share builds, brag runs, coordinate on the community boss.

---

## 3. Season loop (~2–4 weeks)

- The shared **frontier** is a season-long collective objective: chart the
  whole world / fell the season boss / reach the final region.
- **Season end:** did the community make it? Top delvers recognized; the
  finished map becomes a shareable trophy image (the r/place "we did this"
  moment → installs); hero carries over.
- Fresh frontier generates. (Reuses Faction War's season clock + ceremony
  scaffolding.)

---

## 4. Lifetime loop (seasons → forever)

- **Hero persists and grows** across seasons — gear, abilities, cosmetics,
  prestige milestones. This is the "permanent growth" that builds a returning
  player base.
- **Personal service record** (blueprint idea, reused) — deepest delve,
  monsters slain, seasons survived.
- **The sub's history** — frontiers conquered, bosses felled. Reputation, not
  just a reset map. "Maps reset; reputations don't."

---

## The one fork this loop resolves

**Daily-equal (fair, Wordle-clean) vs permanent-growth (retention, player
base).** Resolution baked into the scales above:

- The **delve is instanced & PvE** → a strong hero is your own business; power
  growth is safe there.
- The **competition is co-op** — the sub races *the world* (bosses, fog, the
  season clock), not each other → a strong hero is a gift to the team, never a
  leaderboard-wrecker.
- Any ranking is **contribution / depth** flavored (optionally level-bracketed)
  → friendly bragging without making progression toxic.

## Scaling: thousands of people, one sub

Two layers, so you never cram thousands onto one tactical map:

- **Personal layer (solo/instanced):** your delve is yours (or a 2–4 co-op
  party). Nobody crowds your run.
- **Shared layer (whole sub):** every run's results roll up into ONE community
  meta-objective — a fog map filling in, a boss HP bar thousands chip at, a
  season goal. You share a *scoreboard of progress*, not a screen.
- **Overflow:** a sub too big to share one frontier is **sharded** into
  parallel frontiers (reuses Faction War's "theater" concept).

## Cold start (the "need a player base" worry)

Built PvE/co-op: fun with 3 players, better with 300. Monsters populate the
world, so nobody waits on a crowd for the game to work. The community layer
*adds* value; it's never a prerequisite for a good session.
