# Lore — the mystery spine + every v1 fragment (D50)

Companion to [[WORLD]] (which owns voice/naming/tone; THIS doc owns the
fiction). Fragment-first: players never see a lore wall — only the pieces
below, surfacing through items, encounters, the Codex, and season posts.

## ⚠️ THE TRUTH (spoiler section — for authors, almost never shown)

**It digs back.** The Delve is not a place. It is the *works* of something
enormous that is digging upward, and has been for longer than Torchrest
has existed. Every "depth" is a finished part of its excavation — chambers
it has already passed through. The deeper you go, the closer you are to
where it is currently working.

Canon rules — every fragment must obey ALL of these:

1. **The nightly rearrangement is movement.** The Delve changes daily
   because It progressed in the night. Maps are wrong by morning; the
   daily seed IS this fact
2. **Terrors are heralds, not invaders.** They surface *ahead* of the
   work, the way dust precedes a drill. They do not want Torchrest; they
   are arriving before something that doesn't want anything we understand
3. **Loot is payment — or tailings.** Gear and gold are what the work
   discards (or leaves deliberately; never confirmed which). The Guild
   joke "the Delve pays well" is more true than anyone likes
4. **You can linger where It has been, never where It is.** That is why
   extraction exists, why the wall deepens as It's approached, and why
   monsters are wrong-shaped: they are an ecosystem displaced upward,
   fleeing or following
5. **It is never named, never seen, never confirmed.** Fragments refer
   only to: the work · the sound · the warmth · below. Player-invented
   names become community canon; we never correct them

**Never confirmed, ever** (the questions ARE the content): what It is ·
why It digs · whether It knows about us · whether It can be stopped · how
deep It started · what happens when It arrives.

ground." · Shaman: "Its chant isn't magic. Old-timers call it mimicry — of

**Crypt** · Skeleton: "Buried facing down, every one. Whoever laid these

**Deep** · Wraith: "Delvers who stayed past the tremor, some say. It

**Volcanic** · Magma Imp: "The heat has no volcano under it. The imps just

**Abyss** · Void Spawn: "No face, and doesn't need one. Down here, being
seen isn't how you're found." · Abyss Knight: "Empty armor on patrol over

## Season arc — connected escalation (S1→S3 sketch)

- **Season 1 — "The Gnawing."** Terrors read as an infestation boiling up
  (vermin-things, burrowers). Titan: **Mother of Teeth**. The quiet
  fragment nobody notices yet: they fight *upward through* the Guild, not
  *at* it. They are not hungry. They are ahead of something 🧩
- **Season 2 — "The Procession."** Terrors arrive in ORDER — armored,
  ceremonial, almost patient. Titan: **The Usher**. Fragments: they don't
  fight to win; they fight to *pass* 🧩
- **Season 3 — "The Announcement."** The near-reveal. Titan: **The First
  Word**. Finale fragment (win or lose): *"They were never invading. They
  were announcing."* 🔦 — recontextualizes S1-S2; the community realizes
  the ladder has been a countdown, not a defense. Sets the long game
