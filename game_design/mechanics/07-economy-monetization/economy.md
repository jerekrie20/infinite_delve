# 07 · Economy & Monetization

**Decided** (D2, D16-D18 in [[DECISIONS]]): single currency, four sinks,
strict cosmetics-only monetization.

## Currency (D16)

**Gold only.** No copper/silver/diamond ladder. Short-number formatting
(1.2K / 3.4M) as values grow. Sources: kills, selling gear, idle trickle,
offline expeditions, frontier buff windows.

## The sink ladder (D17-D18)

Ordered by when a player meets them:

| Sink | Purpose | Price shape |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Consumables** | per-run spend decision, push-deeper enabler | cheap, repeatable |
| **Automation tiers** | the marquee sink — the idle game is bought with the idle income | steep steps, milestone-gated ([[core-run]] ladder) |
| **Stash pages** | evergreen QoL | moderate, escalating |
| **Crafting/reroll** | late-game infinite sink, bad-luck protection | scales with rarity + item level |

Design rule: at every stage of account age there is a *current* sink worth
saving for. Idle income should never pool meaninglessly (the pre-plan bug:
gold had zero uses).

## Engagement dial

- **No energy cap** — unlimited personal delves
- **Daily Delve** — the 1×/day appointment (D22)
- **Offline expeditions** — the reason closing the app isn't quitting (D19)
- Reddit Developer Funds (daily qualified engagers + installs) is the
  primary revenue model; the game optimizes honest daily habit, not spend

## Monetization (D2 — strict)

Real money buys **cosmetics + status only**:

- gear skins, hero skins, pets, extraction/celebration effects
- flair, titles
- **community supply drop** — buyer-funded frontier damage boost or sub-wide
  buff day, buyer publicly credited (generosity-as-status; the one purchase
  that touches gameplay, and it's a gift to everyone)

**Never for sale:** gold, automation, stash, consumables, gear, XP, masteries
— nothing on the progression path. If it makes the number go up, it can't be
bought.
